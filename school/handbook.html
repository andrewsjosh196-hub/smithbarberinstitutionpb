School Operations

Rules & Policies

These rules and regulations are designed to help students and instructors carry out our training program. Failure to adhere to these rules may result in dismissal from 1 to 90 days or indefinitely.

Key Policy Areas

  • Drug-Free Policy: Zero tolerance for controlled substances on campus
  • Smoking Policy: Only outside in designated areas
  • Campus Security: 24-hour camera monitoring and alarm system
  • Resource Center: Available during school hours for research and testing
  • Professional Ethics: Expected at all times
  • Sanitation: Strict protocols for health and safety
Core Rules

Rules & Regulations

Drug-Free Policy

Students shall NOT engage in the unlawful distribution, possession, or use of a controlled substance during enrollment. The use or distribution of drugs or alcohol on school premises will result in immediate suspension and/or termination of enrollment.

Smoking Policy

No smoking is allowed inside the building. Smoking is only permitted outside in designated areas.

Campus Security

Smith Barber Institution is monitored 24/7 by camera and connected to local authorities. The school reserves the right to search personal belongings including backpacks, purses, and toolkits.

Resource Center

The school resource center is available during regular hours for:

  • Researching information
  • Making up tests
  • Viewing educational DVDs
  • Filling out paperwork

Important: Do NOT surf the internet or download anything not required for your education. No Facebook, email checking, or personal use.

Professional Ethics

Violations may result in expulsion. Prohibited behaviors include:

  • Use of profanity, alcohol, or drugs during school hours (including lunch)
  • Refusing customers (grounds for suspension)
  • Cheating, stealing, or willful destruction of property
  • Inappropriate phone use while servicing clients
  • Loud conversations disrupting learning
Professional Behavior

Code of Conduct

All students are expected to represent Smith Barber Institution respectfully and professionally.

Professional Standards

You are expected to:

  • Wear proper school uniforms during school hours
  • Have appropriate conversations with fellow students, staff, and clients
  • Avoid sexually explicit language or profanity
  • Conduct yourself as a professional at all times
  • Respect each other, clients, and staff members

Anti-Harassment

Students have the right to learn in an environment free of harassment. If you experience harassment from other students or staff, report it to your instructor or School Administration immediately. Students found engaging in harassment will be expelled.

Anti-Violence

Zero tolerance for violence. Weapons (including knives and firearms) are not permitted on school premises. Students with firearm permits cannot carry on campus. Students making threats or carrying weapons will be expelled.

Visitor Policy

All visitors must check in at the front desk. Visitors are not permitted past the front desk without approval.

Zero Tolerance

The 6 NO's

SBI has zero tolerance policies on the following behaviors:

1. Fighting

Fighting is not allowed. Any involvement in a fight or act of aggression will result in suspension for a minimum of 5 days or expulsion. Students who witness a fight without notifying staff will also face consequences. Play fighting and roughhousing are unacceptable.

2. Threats, Harassment, and Bullying

These behaviors are never tolerated. Consequences depend on severity and may escalate with repeated behavior.

3. Disrespect for Authority

Respect is essential for our community. It is unacceptable to:

  • Argue with staff
  • "Talk back" in a rude tone
  • Act as if rules don't apply to you
  • Refuse to comply with staff requests

If you disagree with a staff decision, follow the chain of command. Consequences increase for repeated disrespect.

4. Leaving Designated Areas

Students may leave campus for lunch breaks but must notify a teacher before leaving any designated area.

5. Stealing or Damaging Property

This violates others' rights and is never acceptable. You will be required to replace, repair, or pay for damaged property, plus face additional consequences.

6. Inappropriate Language

Profanity and vulgar language disrupt learning. Students using inappropriate language will receive appropriate consequences, which may include suspension.

Professional Appearance

Dress Code & Uniform Policy

At Smith Barber Institution, we maintain high standards of professional appearance. The manner in which people first perceive you creates a lasting impression.

Barbering students MUST wear:

  • School-issued smock
  • Completely enclosed shoes (any color)

What You Cannot Wear

  • Hats, headgear, rags, or bandanas
  • Shorts or skirts
  • High heels
  • T-shirts with pictures or writing
  • Sleeveless clothing
  • Flip-flops, open heels, or open-toed shoes
  • Jackets or coats
  • Open cleavage, sagging pants, leggings, or tight-fitting clothes

Important Notes

  • SBI provides ONE smock. Additional smocks must be purchased from the school at additional cost.
  • Uniforms must be clean and neat at all times
  • Soiled uniforms will not be permitted in school
  • Students not properly dressed will be sent home for the day
  • Repeated violations may result in suspension
  • No student will be allowed to clock in without proper uniform
Attendance Requirements

Attendance Policy

Basic Requirements

  • All students are required to attend Saturdays
  • No clock-ins after 10:00 AM Tuesday-Friday
  • No clock-ins after 9:00 AM on Saturdays without permission
  • Strive for 85% or above attendance each month
  • Missing 14 consecutive days without an approved Leave of Absence results in termination

Clock In/Out Procedures

You must:

  • Clock in immediately upon arrival
  • Clock out for a 30-minute lunch break
  • Clock out upon departure
  • Never clock in or out for other students (grounds for suspension/expulsion)

Absent Hours & Overtime Charges

Absent hours are accrued when you:

  • Come to school late
  • Leave school early
  • Take longer than allocated lunch break

Makeup hours remove absent hours. If you don't make up time, overtime charges result at $9.47 per hour, billed monthly. Payment is strictly enforced. If in overtime, disbursements may be held until graduation.

Saturday Charges

Students who miss any Saturday in a 10-week cycle are charged $100.00 for each absence.

Planned Absences

If you know you'll be absent or tardy, submit an "early departure/planned absence" form for approval. For unplanned absences:

  • Call in and speak directly to an available instructor
  • Do NOT leave a message or use voicemail
  • Call at least 30 minutes before your scheduled start time
  • For tardiness, get permission from an instructor (no later than 10:00 AM)

Impact on Financial Aid

Attendance directly affects your financial aid eligibility. Excellent attendance supports your progress and funding.

Academic Progress

Satisfactory Academic Progress

All students receive an academic review monthly. Your satisfactory academic progress (SAP) is monitored to ensure you're succeeding in the program.

SAP Requirements

To maintain satisfactory progress, you must meet these minimums:

  • 67% attendance
  • 75% GPA (both written and practical)

Probation Periods

First Probation: If you're not making satisfactory progress, you're placed on probation. Financial aid continues during this period.

Second Consecutive Probation: If you don't improve, financial aid is suspended. You must make cash payments until progress improves.

Continued Probation: At your current progress rate, you won't graduate by your contract end date. You'll owe additional instructional charges.

Satisfactory Progress Status

If you maintain the required standards, you'll be marked as maintaining satisfactory academic progress.

Students receive numerical grades in theory and practical phases.

Probation

If you fall below 75% overall average in theory or practical, you'll be placed on probation. If satisfactory progress isn't achieved by the end of the probationary period, you may be discontinued from the program.

Completion

Graduation & Licensing Requirements

To Receive a Diploma

You must:

  • Satisfactorily complete your program hours (1500 for Barber/Stylist, 600 for TMI or Crossover)
  • Satisfactorily complete all required practical performances
  • Complete program with minimum 75% GPA (theory and practical) and minimum 67% attendance
  • Pass the school's final written examination with at least 75%
  • Fulfill all contractual and financial obligations

To Be Licensed in Arkansas

After graduation, you must:

  • Receive your diploma from SBI
  • File application for state examination 11 days prior to exam date
  • Pay examination and licensing fees
  • Pass the Arkansas State Board of Barber Examination (written and practical)

Important Notes

  • Transcripts are withheld until all fees and charges are paid
  • Upon graduation, you receive a diploma certifying completion
  • You'll be eligible to apply for the Arkansas State Board Barber license
Curriculum

Programs

Smith Barber Institution offers three comprehensive programs designed to prepare students for barbering careers.

Program Options

  • 1500 Hour Barber/Stylist Program - Traditional barbering training
  • 600 Hour Teacher/Manager/Instructor Program - For advancing to instructor roles
  • 600 Hour Crossover Program - For licensed cosmetologists transitioning to barbering

Hours per Week Requirements

  • Full-time students: Minimum 26 hours/week, maximum 40 hours/week
  • Evening shift students: Minimum 23 hours/week, maximum 40 hours/week
Main Program

Barber/Stylist Program

The 1500-hour Barber/Stylist Program prepares students for the Arkansas State Board Barber examination and professional barbering careers.

Program Structure

  • Total Hours: 1500 clock hours
  • Duration: Typically 12 months for full-time students (30 hrs/week)
  • Fast Track Option: 9 months at 40 hours/week
  • 1 Clock Hour = 60 minutes

Curriculum Overview

Theory (120 hours)

  • History of barbering
  • Professional image and ethics
  • Hygiene and grooming
  • Bacteriology, sterilization, sanitation
  • Barbering implements and techniques
  • Hair cutting (male and female)
  • Shaving and facial treatments
  • Scalp and hair treatments
  • Anatomy and physiology
  • Disorders of skin, scalp, and hair
  • Chemistry
  • Barber shop management
  • Arkansas State Barber Board Laws
  • And more...

Practical Work (1378 hours)

Hands-on training includes:

  • Hair cutting, tapering, trimming, and dressing (800 hours)
  • Shaving (80 hours)
  • Shampooing and rinsing (45 hours)
  • Hair treatments and tonics
  • Bleaching and dyeing
  • Hair styling
  • Facial and massage treatments
  • Professional ethics and shop management
  • Sanitation procedures
  • And more...
Instructor Program

Teacher/Manager/Instructor Program

The Teacher/Manager/Instructor (TMI) 600-hour program is designed for licensed barbers seeking instructor roles.

Program Structure

  • Total Hours: 600 clock hours
  • Duration: Not to exceed 6 continuous months
  • 1 Clock Hour = 60 minutes

Core Curriculum

The TMI course covers:

  • Teaching Techniques & Principles: Methods of instruction, demonstration techniques, lesson planning
  • Classroom Management: Student management, performance evaluation, examination administration
  • Curriculum Development: Subject selection, lecture preparation, learning materials design
  • Advanced Practical Skills Review: Hair cutting, shaving, shampooing, chemical services
  • Preparatory Training: Approximately 50 hours of foundational teaching techniques

Upon Completion

You will receive a diploma certifying completion of the TMI program and qualification for instructor positions.

For Cosmetologists

Crossover Program

The 600-hour Crossover Program is designed for licensed cosmetologists to gain technical barbering skills without repeating shared knowledge.

Program Structure

  • Total Hours: 600 clock hours
  • Requirements: Arkansas requires 600 hours for license
  • Focus: Barbering-specific skills not emphasized in cosmetology

Core Curriculum Focus

Shaving & Facial Massage

  • Straight razor techniques and safety
  • Face and neck shaves using 14-stroke sequence
  • Hot towel application
  • Facial treatments and massage
  • After-shave antiseptic application

Men's Haircutting & Styling

  • Tapering and fading techniques
  • Precision clipper work and blending
  • Beard and mustache design
  • Tool mastery for specialized barbering

Theory & Law

  • History of barbering
  • Arkansas State Barber Board Laws
  • Honing and stropping straight razors
  • Professional ethics for barbers

Upon Completion

You will receive a diploma certifying completion and be eligible to apply for the Arkansas State Board Barber license.
